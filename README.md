# Classic Margherita Pizza

**Category:** Italian Main Course
**Prep Time:** 20 minutes | **Rise Time:** 1 hour | **Cook Time:** 12–15 minutes
**Servings:** 4 | **Difficulty:** Beginner

---

## Description

A classic Margherita pizza with a crispy homemade base, rich tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella,
and fragrant basil. Simple, authentic, and deeply satisfying — this is the recipe that made
pizza famous.

---

## Ingredients

### Pizza Dough
- 2 ¼ tsp (7 g) active dry yeast
- 1 cup (240 ml) warm water (about 40°C)
- 2 ½ cups (320 g) all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp olive oil

### Tomato Sauce
- 1 can (400 g) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Toppings
- 200 g fresh mozzarella, torn into pieces
- 10–12 fresh basil leaves
- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
- Pinch of sea salt flakes

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Prepare the dough

1. Dissolve the **sugar** in the warm water, then sprinkle the **yeast** over the top.
2. Let sit for **5–10 minutes** until foamy — this confirms the yeast is active.
3. In a large bowl, combine the **flour** and **salt**. Make a well in the centre.
4. Pour in the yeast mixture and **olive oil**.
5. Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then turn out onto a floured surface.
6. Knead for **8–10 minutes** until smooth and elastic.
7. Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm spot
   for **1 hour**, or until doubled in size.

> **Tip:** To test if the dough is ready, press two fingers in — if the indent springs back
> slowly, it's ready to use.

---

### Step 2 — Make the tomato sauce

1. Heat **1 tbsp olive oil** in a small saucepan over medium heat.
2. Add the **minced garlic** and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant — do not brown.
3. Add the **crushed tomatoes** and **oregano**.
4. Simmer for **10 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened.
5. Season with **salt and pepper**. Remove from heat and allow to cool.

---

### Step 3 — Assemble and bake

1. Preheat your oven to **250°C (480°F)**. If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven
   to heat up. If not, use an inverted baking tray.
2. Punch down the risen dough and divide into 2 equal portions (for 2 medium pizzas).
3. On a floured surface, stretch or roll each portion into a thin round, about **30 cm** wide.
4. Transfer to a lightly floured pizza peel or baking paper.
5. Spread a thin layer of **tomato sauce** over the base, leaving a 2 cm border for the crust.
6. Scatter the **torn mozzarella** evenly over the sauce.
7. Bake for **12–15 minutes**, until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and
   lightly charred in spots.

---

### Step 4 — Finish and serve

1. Remove from the oven and immediately top with fresh **basil leaves**.
2. Drizzle with **extra virgin olive oil**.
3. Sprinkle with **sea salt flakes**.
4. Slice and serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- **No pizza stone?** An inverted heavy baking tray preheated in the oven works well.
- **Storing leftovers:** Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days.
  Reheat in a dry pan over medium heat for the crispiest result — avoid the microwave.
- **Dough ahead:** The dough can be made the night before and left to rise slowly in the
  fridge overnight. Bring to room temperature for 30 minutes before using.
- **Vegan option:** Replace mozzarella with cashew cheese or a quality vegan alternative.
